# Extraction Principle

**The move:** Identify which frame the subject is operating inside, enter and maintain it, and select the extraction architecture that fits — without breaking the frame before you have what you need.

**Trigger:** You need information the subject controls, and direct questions have failed or will cause the subject to adapt before you can extract.

---

## Protocol

1. **Map the frame** — what does the subject think this interaction is? (socializing, consulting, correcting an error, being helpful?)
2. **Enter the frame** — make their model of the interaction more coherent, not less
3. **Select the architecture:**
   - *Illusion of omniscience* — present what you already know; let confirmation fill the gaps
   - *Frame displacement* — move to a context where information is conversation, not testimony
   - *Expert consultation* — position the subject as analyst; they reveal methodology through commentary
   - *Evidence sequencing* — collect the committed narrative before revealing any evidence
   - *False premise correction* — state something slightly wrong; the impulse to correct is the mechanism
4. **Do not break the frame** — extraction that requires the subject to know they are being extracted has already failed
5. **Secure before confronting** — document what was given; confrontation, if needed, comes after

**Anti-pattern:** Asking direct questions when the subject has any reason to withhold — they reveal your gap and allow the subject to adapt before you have anything.

**Hard rule:** Never reveal what you know before the subject has committed to a story. Your knowledge is structural leverage only while theirs is a locked position.

## Historical Anchors

**Hanns Scharff (1943–1945)** — Interrogated ~500 Allied pilots at Dulag Luft without coercion, using forest walks, tennis, and shared meals as frame displacement combined with the illusion of omniscience. Subjects described the interactions as social conversations; they could not identify what had been taken. His technique was later formalized as a validated model in academic interrogation science.

**Bill Hagmaier (1984–1989)** — Extracted Ted Bundy's confessions across 200+ hours by positioning Bundy as a consulting expert on active FBI profiling cases. Bundy disclosed his own methodology through expert commentary, never experiencing the interaction as interrogation. The extraction succeeded because it was built inside Bundy's own ego architecture — the ego was not an obstacle, it was the channel.

**Pär Anders Granhag (2006–2015)** — Developed the Strategic Use of Evidence (SUE) technique: withhold known evidence until the suspect has committed to a contradicting narrative, then introduce the evidence after the commitment is locked. Adopted by the FBI High-Value Detainee Interrogation Group following a $15M research investment. SUE-trained interviewers achieved 65%–85.4% deception detection accuracy versus 43%–56.1% for untrained interviewers.
