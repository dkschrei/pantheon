# Vessel and Soul Protocol

Invoking Vessel and Soul. Every piece of information has a natural decay rate.

---

## The Decay Rate Question

> **"What is the natural lifespan of this information?"**

| Answer | Tier | Action |
|--------|------|--------|
| Hours to days | **Fleeting** | Capture quickly. Set a promotion deadline. Promote or discard. |
| Years to forever | **Permanent** | Distill the insight. Strip context. Store durably. |
| Milestone event | **Immutable** | Append to audit trail. Never edit. |

---

## What happens now

1. **Ask the decay rate** — hours-to-days, years-forever, or milestone-event?
2. **Route to the matching tier** — do not mix
3. **For fleeting:** decide now — when will you promote or discard?
4. **For permanent:** distill first — "what would I need to know in five years with no memory of today?"
5. **For immutable:** append only — the event happened; do not revise history
6. **Check the conversation window** — anything that must survive this session must be promoted before it ends

---

## The Hard Rule

The conversation window is a vessel, not a soul. It is a processing layer, not a storage tier. Any information that must survive beyond this session must be extracted and promoted now — or it is lost when the vessel is discarded.

---

## Anti-pattern to avoid

The single-bucket trap: treating all information as equally permanent (noise fills signal) or equally ephemeral (wisdom evaporates). The most dangerous version: using the conversation as the only storage tier — everything lives there until it doesn't.
