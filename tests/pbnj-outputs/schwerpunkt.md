# PB&J Test — schwerpunkt

**Input:** Construct a Peanut Butter and Jelly Sandwich

**Output:**

## Schwerpunkt: PB&J

**Step 1 — Map the system.**

Nodes: bread selection, knife, peanut butter spread, jelly spread, slice combination, cut/serve. Dependencies: spread quality depends on knife; combination depends on both spreads; every subsequent step depends on bread integrity.

**Step 2 — Find the center of gravity.**

Remove each node mentally. Bad jelly spread? Manageable — jelly is thin and self-levels when pressed. Wrong bread? Annoying but workable. **Torn bread from aggressive peanut butter spreading? The sandwich collapses. Everything downstream fails.**

The center of gravity is **the peanut butter spread on slice one.** PB is viscous, cold-resistant, and tears bread if rushed. It's load-bearing for structural integrity, even coverage, and binding the halves.

**Step 3 — Holding forces.**

Jelly slice gets minimum viable effort: one pass, edge-to-edge, done. Don't overthink it.

**Step 4 — Concentrate best resources at the decisive node.**

Use the widest knife. Let PB reach room temp if possible. Spread from center outward with deliberate strokes. Full coverage to edges.

**Step 5 — Strike fast, exploit the collapse.**

Once PB is set, jelly goes in seconds. Press, cut, done. The hard problem already fell — everything else resolves automatically.

**The anti-pattern:** spreading jelly and PB simultaneously on both slices. Uniform pressure. Slower, messier, more likely to tear.
